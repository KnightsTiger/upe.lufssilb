Bird Watching Tour

09 Nights/ 10 Days Tour

Day 01

On Arrival at Bandaranayake International airport you will be welcomed by our representative Drive towards Anuradhapura Accommodation at Anuradhapura

Day 02


Early morning with packets of breakfast leave for Wilpattu National park jeep safari. Wilpattu national park in Sri Lanka has the most leopard density when compared to other national parks, also the national park is among the top national parks in the world for its leopard population, Wilpattu national park is extremely high biodiversity, including 31 species of mammals, sloth bear (Melursus Ursinus inornatus), water buffalo (Bubalus bubalis), elephant (Elephas Maximus Maximus), Sambhur (Rusa unicolor unicolor), Spotted deer (Axis axis ceylonensis), Mongoose, and shrew are most known Drive towards Sigiriya Accommodation at Sigiriya

Day 03


Early morning with packets of breakfast leave for Sigiriya Sanctuary to watch various birds Surrounding of Sigiriya Rock Fortress is a designated forest reserve and is a very good birding destination. Sigiriya sanctuary is a well-protected area as it is a UNECO World Heritage Site, waterways and thick canopied forests make it a heaven for birds. You will be accompanied by our expertbird watching naturalist for the birding trip in Sigiriya Sanctuary in the morning hours from 6 Am to 10 AM Afternoon jeep safari to Minnariya national park to witness the elephants & other wild life species – Minnaeriya national park is the largest known meeting place of Asian Elephants in the world. During this period herds up to 300 elephants are seen at the 8,890 hectare park within a few square kilometers of the Minneriya Reservoir. In August and September each year during the dry season, wild elephants from the surrounding wilderness in search of food and water, makes their way to the shores of the Minneriya Reservoir adjoining the Minneriya National Park. Huge herd of elephants, sometimes numbering up to 300, converge together within a few square kilometers of the lake Accommodation at Sigiriya

Day 04


Breakfast at the hotel & Drive towards Kandy En route visits Dambulla cave temple & Spice garden in Matale Sightseeing around Kandy Afternoon enjoy traditional Kandyan dances & visit Teplme of tooth in Kandy Accommodation at Kandy

Day 05


Morning with packet of breakfast nature trail to Udawatta Forest Udawatte Kele is the only forest reserve in the Kandy city limits and is a great addition to your list of things to do in Kandy. This breathtaking forest is a hotspot for bird enthusiasts. This historic sanctuary is famous not only for its avifauna but also its variety of plant species including lianas, shrubs and small trees. It contains 460 plant species including 135 species of trees and nine endemic species. You will be able to see the historically important ruins and places from the Kandyan Kingdom such as the Lady Horton’s Road, Royal Pond, Lovers’ Walk and Marble Seat Drive towards Nuwara Eliya On the way visit Ramboda waterfall thereafter visit the Tea Factory Planation to witness the process Accommodation at Nuwara Eliya

Day 06


Early morning with packets of breakfast visit to the Horton Plains National Park Return to the hotel Accommodation at Nuwara Eliya

Day 07


Breakfast at the hotel Drive towards Sinharaja Accommodation at Sinharaja

Day 08


Early Full day bird watching in Sinharaja Rain Forest in search for birds such as Munias, Flower peckkers, sunbirds, fly catchers, Mynas, Babblers, Monarchs, Wabblers, Prinias, Bul Buls, Wagtails, Drongos, Orioles, Leafbirds, Woodpeckers, barbets, Pigeons, Nuthatchs, Trogans, Hornbills, Blue Magpie, Frogmouths, and Coucals etc Accommodation at Sinharaja

Day 09


Breakfast at the hotel & proceed towards Colombo Afternoon visit Beddagana Wetland park - The Beddagana urban wetland, which is part of the flood plans of the Diyawanna Lake, is part of a larger Wildlife Sanctuary declared by the Department of Wildlife Conservation in 1985. It became the location of a rich and flourishing ecosystem as many birds and animals made their home amongst the wilderness. Untouched by humans and unseen from prying eyes, the marshland became a haven for rare local and migratory birds, and a large number of insect life. Seeds brought in by the birds also gave rise to many beautiful and rare flowering plants, causing a number of butterfly species to visit the area. Accommodation at Colombo

Day 10


Breakfast at the hotel Departure transfer to the airport
